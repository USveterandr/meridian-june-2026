## Why the Dominican Republic?

Before the numbers, the fundamentals:

**Tourism is the engine.** The DR welcomed 10.3 million tourists in 2024 — a Caribbean record. That's more than Jamaica, Puerto Rico, and the Bahamas combined. Tourism directly drives short-term rental demand, and that demand has pushed occupancy rates above 70% in peak season in markets like Punta Cana and Cap Cana.

**The dollar is dominant.** Real estate in the DR is priced and transacted in USD. This protects foreign investors from peso devaluation and makes valuation straightforward.

**GDP growth.** The DR has averaged over 5% GDP growth for the past decade, consistently outperforming every other Latin American and Caribbean economy. Growth drives long-term property appreciation.

**Infrastructure investment.** The government has poured billions into roads, airports, and coastal developments. Punta Cana International Airport is expanding. A new marina at Las Terrenas opened in 2024. Cap Cana's private airport handles private jets year-round.

## Short-Term vs. Long-Term Rental

**Short-term (Airbnb/VRBO):** Higher gross revenue. More management-intensive. Best in Punta Cana, Cap Cana, Las Terrenas. Many resort-zone properties have on-site management programs that handle guests, cleaning, and booking.

**Long-term:** Steadier income, less management. Best in Santo Domingo (professional tenant base) and cities with growing expat populations. Typical lease: 1 year, paid monthly in USD or DOP. Yields are slightly lower (6–8%) but more predictable.

Many investors start short-term, then switch to long-term after the property appreciates — capturing the best of both strategies.

## How to Finance DR Property as a Foreigner

**All cash:** The most common method for foreign buyers. Eliminates financing risk, faster closing, often better negotiating position.

**Developer financing:** Many new developments offer seller financing at 8–12% interest with 20–30% down. Typical terms: 3–5 years. Best for pre-construction properties.

**DR mortgage (rare for foreigners):** A few Dominican banks offer mortgages to foreign nationals with strong credit history. Rates are 10–14% in DOP or 6–9% in USD. Requires significant documentation. Most foreign buyers don't use this route.

**Home equity / HELOC from your US property:** Many investors leverage existing US home equity to fund DR acquisitions. This is increasingly common among US-based Dominican diaspora buyers.

## Tax Implications for Foreign Investors

**In the Dominican Republic:**
- **Annual property tax (IPI):** 1% on the assessed value above ~$155K USD. Tourism-zone properties may qualify for Law 158-01 exemptions.
- **Rental income tax:** 27% on net rental income (income minus allowable expenses). Many investors structure through a local entity (SRL) for efficiency.
- **Capital gains:** 27% on profits from sale. Primary residences are exempt.

**In your home country (US investors):**
- DR rental income is reportable on your US taxes as foreign rental income
- You can deduct DR expenses, depreciation, and taxes against US income
- FATCA reporting applies if you have >$50K in foreign assets
- Consider a 1031 exchange if selling a US property to fund the DR acquisition

*Always consult a cross-border tax advisor. This is informational, not tax advice.*

## Law 158-01: The Tourism Investment Incentive

One of the DR's most powerful investor tools. Law 158-01 grants qualified tourism-zone investors:

- **Exemption from import duties** on construction materials and equipment
- **IPI (property tax) exemption** for 10 years
- **Income tax exemption** on rental profits for 10 years
- **Tax exemption on capital gains** from the sale for 10 years

This applies to qualifying new developments and hotels in designated tourism zones (most of Punta Cana, Cap Cana, Samaná, and other certified areas). Many developments marketed on Meridian qualify — look for the "Law 158-01 Eligible" tag.

## Pre-Construction vs. Resale

**Pre-construction (planos):**
- 10–20% below completed market value at signing
- Appreciation locked in during construction (typically 18–36 months)
- Developer financing often available
- Risk: developer delays or failures (mitigated by working with established developers)

**Resale:**
- What you see is what you get — no construction uncertainty
- Immediate rental income potential
- Negotiable price, especially in softer markets
- Requires more due diligence on condition and history

For investors with a 3–5 year horizon, pre-construction in Cap Cana or Punta Cana has generated the highest total returns over the last decade.
